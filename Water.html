Water is essential to life, and it is important to understand how it works.

Polarity

Water is a polar molecule with intramolecular covalent bonding and intermolecular hydrogen bonding.

Covalent bonds between oxygen and hydrogen IN the water molecule.

- Hydrogen bonds between oxygen and hydrogen BETWEEN neighboring water molecules

Universal Solvent

Partial negative oxygen in water can bind with other, partially-positive molecules.

Partial positive hydrogen in water can bind with other, partially-negative molecules.

Cohesion & Adhesion

Cohesion - attraction of molecules of the same kind (Water attracted to water.)

Adhesion - attraction of molecules of a different kind (Water attracted to other polar molecules.)

Surface Tension

Cohesion develops a "surface" atop water bodies due to hydrogen bonding.

Density

Water is less dense as a solid since hydrogen bonds inhibit compaction, so ice floats in liquid water.
